Research

Dive into the science and challenges of alternative proteins.

The Promise of Alternative Proteins

Alternative proteins reduce emissions by up to 93% and use less land and water. Plant-based options like pea and soy are scalable, but cultured meat faces regulatory hurdles. These challenges drive consumer adoption issues.

References: Onwezen, M. C., et al. (2024). Consumer Acceptance. MDPI.; Nature. (2024). Challenges of Alt Proteins.

Consumer Adoption Barriers

Despite benefits in The Promise, taste, price, and cultural norms hinder acceptance. 68% of consumers see plant-based meat as healthier, but concerns persist. See Strategies for solutions.

References: Deloitte. (2024). Alt Proteins Reality Check.; Onwezen, M. C., et al. (2024). Consumer Acceptance Re-Review.

Strategies for Market Expansion

Overcoming barriers requires taste parity and localized marketing. Blockchain traceability and AI enhance appeal, aligning with alt protein potential. Public investment aids scalability.

References: ScienceDirect. (2024). Market Opportunities.; Agilent. (2024). Future of Food.
